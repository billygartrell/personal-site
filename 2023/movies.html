Thoughts

Wow, I watched a lot more movies than I thought this year. I shifted a lot of my ‘hang out on the couch and relax time’ from watching tv shows to watching movies. And honestly, it’s the right move. They're shorter. More digestable. Less fluff. There are two major things that were a bit different this year that I really loved.

Seasonality

Laura and I watched a lot of movies depending on the season. Perhaps we were inspired from our friends Corey and Lauren who watched a movie all 31 days during October. We wound up doing something similar. This year, fall was perfect. We went apple picking, we cooked good soups, had some pumpking spice lattes, went to the Renaissance Faire and capped most of our nights with classic halloween movies. It made me appreciate the season more than ever. I never really considered the mood-setting capabilities of film before this year. It rules

Connected Art

Last year, I noted that I wanted to watch films from my favorite directors. As the year was closing, I realized that I hadn’t done this. So, I started to crank through Danny Boyle films. It was so much fun. It’s great seeing an artist's vision come to life. When I find a band I love, I’ll get into a record and then start to dive through their back catalogue. And now, I feel that equivalent for film. Seeing how an director circles themes, explores stories and grows over time is wonderful. And it’s inspiring for my own work even if I have no interest in making a film in my life. Not only is there an interconnectedness among their own work, but across the dicipline and other media. Here's an example of how I noticed the interconnectitity this year: I was reading Murakami. I wanted to find other artists who do work like him. I read an article about directors who were inspired by Murakami. I found the Chinese director, Wong Kar Wai. I watched his movie Fallen Angels. I read another article about how Sophia Coppola was inspired by his work. I watcher her film Lost in Translation. I got to follow a thread from a different art form into film and see how all of this work is interconnected. It's the best.

I watched a lot of classics this year, too! I don’t need to write any more words about this but film was one of my favorite things I explored this year. It’s my second favorite art form behind music. Confirmed.
