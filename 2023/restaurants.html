Thoughts

I'm truly so thankful for the food that I get to eat. It's one of my favorite things to explore by myself or with others. And once again, I've gotten to try many, many different places this year, largely without trying. I'm really lucky. I just learned of the Japanese phrase "itadakimasu" a few days ago. It translates to "to humbly receive." Japanese people will say it before eating a meal to give honor to all that went into the meal: the natural ingredients, the people who grew them, the ones who prepared them. I think it's a wonderful phrase. After seeing this kind of insane list and how much I spend on new eating experiences, I'm going to start carrying this phrase with me.

Last year I learned that I loved Japanese food and this year I explored it. I also checked out a lot of Korean food this year too. It's all amazing. I have no more thoughts other than that. I want more. It's insatiable.

Laura and I went to Europe this year and had some incredible meals there. I know it's a joke that English food is unseasoned and not great, or whatever, but the best meals I had all year were in the English countryside. Don't listen to the haters.
